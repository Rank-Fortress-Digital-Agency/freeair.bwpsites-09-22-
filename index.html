Ways to improve your furnace's energy efficiency


Your furnace is one of the essential appliances in your home, and it’s also one of the biggest energy consumers. So it’s necessary to ensure you’re doing everything you can to improve its energy efficiency.

Get your furnace serviced regularly.


One of the best ways to keep your furnace running efficiently is to get it serviced regularly. This will help to ensure that all the parts are in good working order and that there are no potential problems that could cause your furnace to use more energy than necessary.

Servicing your furnace regularly will also help to improve its lifespan, as any potential issues will be caught and addressed before they have a chance to cause severe damage. A service technician will check during a routine servicing, including the burner, the heat exchanger, the blower, and the filters. They will also inspect all of the ductwork to ensure that there are no leaks or blockages.

If you have not had your furnace serviced in a while, it is essential to do so as soon as possible. A professional service technician can usually provide a thorough inspection and tune-up for a reasonable price, so there is no excuse not to keep your furnace running smoothly. Schedule an appointment today and start enjoying the benefits of a well-maintained furnace.

Replace your furnace filter regularly


Another simple way to improve your furnace’s energy efficiency is to replace the filter regularly. A dirty filter can restrict airflow and make your furnace work harder, which uses more energy. Depending on your filter type, you may need to replace it every few months.

You can also save energy by using a programmable thermostat to control your furnace. A programmable thermostat can save you money by automatically turning down the heat when you don’t need it, such as when you’re asleep or away from home.

Finally, make sure your furnace is properly maintained. A well-maintained furnace will run more efficiently and use less energy. Be sure to have your furnace serviced by a qualified technician at least once a year.

Use a programmable thermostat.


If you have a central heating system, using a programmable thermostat can help you save energy by automatically turning your furnace off when you’re not home and turning it back on before you need it. Consider upgrading to a more efficient model.


Fortunately, there are many ways to improve the energy efficiency of your furnace and get more heat for your money. One of the simplest and most cost-effective is to upgrade to a newer, more efficient model. Many more recent models are up to 95% efficient, saving you hundreds of dollars per year on your heating bill.

Other ways to improve energy efficiency include proper maintenance and cleaning of your furnace filters, ensuring that all registers and vents in your home are open and unobstructed, using programmable thermostats to optimize heating schedules, and making sure that your home is adequately insulated. These simple steps can save money and keep your home warm and comfortable all winter long.

Insulate your home


Another critical factor in furnace efficiency is how well insulated your home is. If heat can escape through cracks or gaps in your home’s envelope, your furnace will have to work harder to maintain a comfortable temperature, which uses more energy. Adding more insulation to your home can help to reduce heat loss and improve your furnace’s efficiency.

Other ways to improve furnace efficiency include:

– Ensuring that your furnace is the correct size for your home. If it is too small, it will have to work harder to heat your home, and if it is too large, it will cycle on and off more frequently, using more energy.


– Maintaining your furnace and having it serviced regularly. A well-maintained furnace will operate more efficiently than one that is not. Regular servicing can also help to identify potential problems before they cause your furnace to break down.

– Using a programmable thermostat to control the temperature in your home better. This can help to reduce energy use when you are not home or when you are asleep.

By taking these steps, you can help to improve your furnace’s efficiency and reduce your energy costs.

Seal ductwork leaks


If you have a central heating and cooling system, it’s essential to ensure that the ductwork is sealed correctly. Leaks in the ductwork can allow heated or cooled air to escape, which causes your furnace or air conditioner to work harder and use more energy. Having your ductwork professionally sealed can help improve your HVAC system’s efficiency and save you money on your energy bills.

If you’re unsure if your ductwork is sealed correctly, you can hire a professional to inspect it and make any necessary repairs. You can also check for leaks yourself by looking for signs of gaps or cracks in the ductwork. If you find any leaks, you can seal them with mastic sealant or foil tape.

Ensuring your ductwork is an integral part of maintaining your HVAC system. By taking the time to inspect and repair any leaks, you can help improve the efficiency of your system and save money on your energy bills.
